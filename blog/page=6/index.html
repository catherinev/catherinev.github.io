My thoughts on the design and usability of a few websites

Serious Eats

Serious Eats is one of my favorite websites. Like so many other websites, it has an excellent assortment of delicious recipes and reviews of restaurants all over the world. What sets it apart from other websites for me is primarily Kenji, one of the blog writers who has a background in science and writes excellent (and sometimes surprising) articles on the science behind his recipes. There are other fantastic contributors to the website, but I especially enjoy reading about Kenji's experimental process and, at the same time, learning some food science. Who doesn't want to know why their food tastes good?

I find the user-experience to be quite enjoyable and the design to be streamlined and easy to look at. Like any good food website, the homepage is full of photos of food, to which a user's eyes are immediately drawn. The colors are light and undistracting, and there is an appropriate amount of contrast between text and backgrounds.
