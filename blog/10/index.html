Tuesday, April 22, 2014

Affirmation and Stereotype Threat

One reason I haven't felt particularly threatened by stereotypes is that I value personal achievement, improvement, and challenge above what other people think of me. Thus, it's easy to disregard stereotypes in favor of more objective measures of my ability and potential.

I also strongly value the close relationships I have with others. During times of stress, I find comfort in such relationships not because of any encouraging words or favorable opinions but rather because of the shared experiences, closeness, and comfort they provide. I have been able to work through some of my most trying moments by helping others work through their own challenges and achieve their own successes.

In moments of doubt at DBC, I think it will be important for me to focus on a few things. First, I shouldn't compare myself to others: I have my own standards for myself, and everyone at DBC has a different background and different goals. Instead of focusing on other people, I should focus on achieving mymaximum potential in the time we have. Second, I should honestly evaluate myself. What have I done well? What is causing my self-doubt? Is it fair to judge myself so harshly for struggling with such a concept? What can I do to move past this moment? The last thing I should keep in mind is that I have (and will hopefully continue to build) strong relationships on which I can rely for support.

My goal is to get as much out of DBC as I personally am able to. Through the prep phase and Phase 0 (thus far), I have had some moments when I felt great and totally confident in my ability to learn so much material so quickly and other moments when I felt overwhelmed, incompetent, and inadequate. But so far I've been able to overcome the tough moments, and as long as I do my best and evaluate myself according to my own standards, I'm sure I'll continue to succeed.
