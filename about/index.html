About me

I'm a former mathematician and now a software engineer working at Narrative Science. Our platform, Quill, takes structured data and turns it into natural language; for example, we convert baseball statistics into engaging articles about the games. I started on the client team where I wrote self-contained applications that extracted key insights from client data. Now I've transitioned to the team that is rebuilding Quill from the ground up.

I have a masters degree in math from the University of Illinois at Chicago. For more than 10 years, I taught math to students of all ages and levels. I enjoy applying a lifetime of technical, communications, and management skills to software development.

In my spare time, I like to eat and experiment with food. I especially like cheese and chocolate (but not together). Sometimes I run, but not very far. I also like to make things like quilts.
